Air Dancer

Model an air dancer with verlet integration.

An air dancer can be modeled with a set of connected circles. To reproduce their movement while maintaining their connectivity, we can simplify Newton's formula to this \begin{align*} &p_{new}(i) = 2p_{curr}(i)-p_{old}(i)+a\Delta t\\ &p_{old}(i) = p_{curr}(i) \end{align*} where the $p(i)$'s are the new, current and old positions of the $i$th circles. The velocity is replaced by $p_{curr}(i)-p_{old}(i)$. In the real world, this may offend some physicists. But in a computer simulation where time is discretize, air dancers don't need to obey physic law; and those who don't are privilege to gain some computational efficiency.

The distance between two connected circles is constrained by a certain bound so that the body of the air dancer stays as a whole. If during the position-updating process a constrain is violated, then we need to iterate a readjustment policy until there is no more violation. \begin{align*} &d = distance(i,i+1)\\ &p_{curr}(i) = p_{curr}(i) + d/2\\ &p_{curr}(i+1) = p_{curr}(i+1) - d/2 \end{align*}

And that's basically verlet integration!

Next, we consider the acceleration of the air dancer because the first equation has an $a$ term. We can model this acceleration as function of sine and cosine to create the wave pattern.

Finally we add some facial characteristics in an attempt to amplify the emotional aspect of dance.
